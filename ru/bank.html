LibreBank

What

LibreBank is the 1st Liquidity smart contract for trustless bond pooling. With LibreBank you can earn more TZL compared to traditional delegation.

Who

Any XTZ holder can deposit in the LibreBank and earn TZL.

Why

Delegating via LibreBank is more profitable than standard delegation. LibreBank helps TzLibre scale its baking operations, which is key to override KYC on KYC-Tezos and to raise seed funding to bootstrap TzLibre.

How

LibreBank is a Liquidity smart contract deployed on the KYC-Tezos network at the address KT1V7VoyjbvqSmnRtv9pHkRuBCPT7UubCrCX. It allows XTZ holders to deposit and withdraw XTZ at any time.

Reward

  • Current interest: 5%/month
  • Fixed TZL price: 0.033 XTZ
  • Reward currency: TZL
  • Payout schedule: monthly
  • Example reward: Depositing 1000 XTZ will generate 1500 TZL / month
  • Rewards are paid out to the linked Ethereum address

Terms

  • Minimum deposit (per account): 1,000 XTZ
  • Maximum deposit (per account): n/a
  • Maximum deposits (global, October): 500,000 XTZ
  • Deposit fee: 0.10 XTZ
  • Withdrawal fee: 0.1%
  • Maximum borrow: 75%

RoI

With a 5%/month reward, LibreBank deposits are:

  • 5x more profitable than delegating TzLibre with a traditional delegation
  • 5.6x more profitable than the best KYC-Tezos delegate (5% fee)

How it works

The smart contract keeps a minimum collateral by allowing TzLibre to only withdraw up to 75% of deposited funds (this is hardcoded in the smart contract). Thanks to this guarantee depositors can withdraw their own deposit at any given time. When a depositor withdraws, TzLibre subsequently withdraws an equal amount of its baking bond in order to add collateral to the smart contract.

Wallet setup

You must use a wallet that allows interacting with KYC-Tezos smart contracts. Currently only two wallets allow that:

  • tezos-client (expert users)
  • LibreBox (normal users)

How to deposit

  1. Check you are using a smart contract enabled wallet (see above) and that you have enough balance (minimum 1002 XTZ). If the wallet is not funded, move funds from your KT1 address (e.g. the address you're currently delegating TzLibre with) or tz1 address in order to fund it
  2. Deposit: send your funds (minimum 1000 XTZ) to the smart contract address KT1V7VoyjbvqSmnRtv9pHkRuBCPT7UubCrCX and always keep some balance (minimum 2 XTZ) inside the wallet
  3. Check your deposit was successful: your wallet balance will decrease by the deposited amount and you'll find the deposit in your transaction history

How to withdraw

  1. Send 1 XTZ to the smart contract address KT1V7VoyjbvqSmnRtv9pHkRuBCPT7UubCrCX
  2. Check your withdrawal was successful: your wallet balance will grow by the withdrawn amount and you'll find the withdrawal in your transaction history

More info

The purpose of LibreBank is to allow for trustless lending of XTZ to increase TzLibre baking bond. By capping the borrowable amount and by spreading the remaining counterparty risk among multiple depositors, this smart contract allows users to withdraw their own deposit at any time. TzLibre will borrow XTZ from the contract in order to increase its baking bonds. Larger baking bonds allow TzLibre to accept an ever growing number of delegations.

Current status

A summary of LibreBank's status (total deposits, collateral) is shown in our header bar. For more information about the contract state, open its TzScan page and go to: Code -> Storage -> Michelson storage. You'll find the whole contract state there, in this order:

  • Constants: max depositors, min deposit*, withdraw fee %*, min collateral*
  • Variables: tot deposited*, tot borrowed*, deposits map (address, balance*), map size. (*fixed point values, multiplied by 10^6 as per Tezos standard)

Verify

We encourage all users to verify our Liquidity source compiles to the deployed contract:

  1. Open the Github repository
  2. Find the Liquidity source at libre-bank.liq
  3. Compile it into Michelson (you could use a web tool)
  4. Verify the compiled Michelson code is the same as the Michelson code deployed on KYC-Tezos ("Code" section on TzScan)
